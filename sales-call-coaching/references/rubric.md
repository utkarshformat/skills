# Scoring Rubric — Behavioural Anchors

Score each conversation 0 / 1 / 2 on each dimension. Anchors describe **observable behaviour in the transcript**, not impressions. When a call genuinely gives no opportunity to demonstrate a dimension (e.g. hung up before any pitch was possible, a pure scheduling/holding call, or a correct disqualification), mark it **N/A** and exclude it from that dimension's average rather than scoring 0.

**Disqualification carve-out.** If the prospect reveals a genuine, fit-based non-fit (a setup the product can't serve, an equivalent owned/contracted solution they're committed to, a hard ICP miss) and the rep ends the call efficiently, that is a **correct disqualification, not a cave**: mark **Objection handling** and **Next step** N/A and keep the call out of coaching. Timing / budget / political stalls ("new leadership settling in", "not this fiscal year", "we're mid-contract") are **stalls, not disqualifiers** — score them normally.

**Display:** every dimension score is shown as `X/2` (`0/2`, `1/2`, `2/2`). The per-call total is shown as `X.X/2.0`.

**Weighted call score** = Σ(dimension score × weight), over the dimensions that were scored (re-normalise across non-N/A weights if any dimension is N/A). Weights sum to 1.0 and each score is 0–2, so the total lands on a **0.0–2.0** scale. Express it as `X.X/2.0`. (Worked example: `0.10×2 + 0.10×2 + 0.20×2 + 0.15×2 + 0.20×1 + 0.20×1 + 0.05×2 = 1.60`, shown as `1.60/2.0`.)

## 1. Opening & permission — weight 10%
Does the rep open cleanly, name who they are, and earn the right to keep talking?

- **0** — No name/company given, or launches into pitch with no framing; gets shut down or talked over immediately.
- **1** — Names self/company but rushes past permission; prospect is mildly disoriented about who's calling and why.
- **2** — Names self + company, references prior contact if any, and the prospect is oriented and willing to continue. Survives the gatekeeper without friction.

## 2. Reason-for-call clarity — weight 10%
Is it clear, early, why the rep is calling?

- **0** — Reason never stated plainly, or buried so late the prospect is confused throughout.
- **1** — Reason given but vague or hedged ("I just wanted to touch base").
- **2** — Clear, specific reason stated in the first turn or two ("I'm calling to see whether [specific problem] is still costing you [specific impact]").

## 3. Discovery / pain — weight 20%
Does the rep uncover the prospect's real situation and pain, or talk at them?

- **0** — No real questions; rep monologues; no information gathered about current process, metrics, or who decides.
- **1** — Asks 1–2 surface questions, accepts the first answer, doesn't probe ("ah ok, got it" and moves on).
- **2** — Asks about the current process, quantifies the problem (volumes, error rates, cost, time), and probes who decides and on what timeline. Threads the prospect's answers into follow-ups.

## 4. Value framing — weight 15%
Is the offering tied to *this* prospect's situation, or a canned pitch?

- **0** — Generic feature dump with no link to anything the prospect said.
- **1** — Explains the offering competently but generically; some link to the prospect's context but mostly standard.
- **2** — Explicitly maps the offering to the prospect's stated pain ("since you said [their situation], the part that matters for you is [specific capability]") and references comparable customers/situations.

## 5. Objection handling — weight 20%
When the prospect brushes off, does the rep explore and reframe, or cave / steamroll?

Common brush-off families: "we already have a vendor / it's outsourced", "no budget right now", "not this fiscal year / call me next quarter", "we're focused on other priorities."

**Watch for the timing/budget/political stall family — it's usually structural, not a per-rep weakness.** Newly-appointed decision-makers needing time to settle, budget freezes, and contract-renewal timing ("call us back in the autumn", "not until next year") recur across reps because they're features of the market, not failures of the individual. When scoring, watch specifically for *how* the rep handles this family. If the *same* family recurs across multiple reps, surface it in the patterns section as a team enablement gap (one shared rebuttal to build and drill), not several separate coaching items. A reframe that scores 2 here usually converts the stall into a low-commitment next step the prospect can say yes to *now* despite the timing.

- **0** — Caves instantly ("ok, no problem") and moves to wrap, OR argues/steamrolls without acknowledging.
- **1** — Acknowledges the objection and gives a reasonable response, but doesn't explore the root ("why next year? what triggers the decision?").
- **2** — Acknowledges, asks a clarifying question to understand the real constraint, then reframes with relevant proof or a lower-commitment ask (e.g. "even if the decision is in Q4, let's book 30 minutes so you have the context ready when it comes up").

## 6. Next step secured — weight 20%
Does the call end with a real, dated next step?

- **0** — No next step, or "I'll send some documents / you'll let me know."
- **1** — A next step proposed but soft and undated ("let's reconnect sometime", "maybe").
- **2** — A concrete, dated commitment with mutual agreement ("let's book 30 minutes next week"), or a clear, time-bound agreed follow-up the prospect assented to.

## 7. Call control & register — weight 5%
Does the rep guide the call and hold a professional register?

- **0** — Prospect drives throughout; rep reactive; register too casual or fumbling.
- **1** — Mostly guides the call; occasional loss of thread; register acceptable.
- **2** — Steers the call start to finish, handles the gatekeeper smoothly, professional and warm register throughout.

---

## Scoring discipline
- Score what's **in the transcript**, not what you assume the rep meant.
- A short call is not automatically a low score — a crisp 3-minute call that earns a dated next step can score high.
- A long call is not automatically a high score — rambling counts against discovery and control.
- When in doubt between two scores, pick the lower and note why; the point is honest coaching signal, not flattering averages.
