# Repurposing Guide

## The Atoms

Before making derivative assets, extract the reusable pieces from your case study:

- 2-3 headline metrics
- 3-5 quotable customer lines
- 1 before/after transformation
- 1 aha moment story
- 1 competitive displacement detail (if applicable)
- 1 objection the customer overcame

Every asset you make pulls from these atoms.

---

## Asset List (Priority Order)

### Must-Haves

**Full case study** — Website page, 800-1200 words, full structure.

**One-pager PDF** — The asset your sales rep sends after a call without explanation.

```
TOP: Customer logo + descriptor ("Series B, 80-person team") + one-line summary
LEFT: Challenge (3 bullets) + Why they chose you (1-2 bullets)
RIGHT: Results (3-4 metrics) + Hero quote with headshot
BOTTOM: How it works (3 bullets) + CTA
```

**Proof slide** — One slide for the sales deck. Logo, 1-2 metrics, one quote, one-sentence headline. Must work when screenshotted and pasted into Slack.

**LinkedIn posts (3-5)** — Space these over 2-4 weeks:
1. Narrative story post (150-250 words, founder POV, tag the customer)
2. Stat card (image: big number + logo)
3. Customer shout-out (tag the champion, they'll reshare)
4. "How they did it" carousel (6-8 slides)
5. Quote + your commentary

**Cold outreach snippet** — One proof point per email, extreme brevity:

> "We helped a VP CS at a 120-person SaaS shop cut onboarding time by 40% in 3 months. They were in a similar spot to you."

### Nice-to-Haves

**Blog post** — Retell the story for SEO. More editorial, less structured.

**Email nurture story** — 300-400 words in a drip sequence. CTA to the full story.

**Video/audio clip** — 15-60 seconds of the best interview moment. Captions, stat callout, works on mute.

**Ad creative** — LinkedIn image ad: logo + metric + "See how [Company] did it →"

**Multi-customer compilation** — "3 companies that cut churn by 30%." Great thought leadership piece.

---

## Cold Outreach Patterns

**Metric + similarity:**
> "We helped [Company] (Series A, 60 employees, heavy call volume) cut reporting time by 40%. You remind me a lot of them."

**Quote + context:**
> "[Customer]'s Head of Product told us: 'We had recordings nobody was watching.' Sound familiar?"

**Before/after:**
> "Before [Product], [Customer] spent 6 hours a week on manual reviews. Now they get a weekly digest in 15 minutes."

Put these in: opening lines, P.S. lines, follow-up emails, or email signatures.

---

## Distribution Sequence

**Week 1:** Publish the page. Send one-pager + slide to Sales. First LinkedIn post (tag customer). Add logo to homepage.

**Week 2:** Stat card post. Customer shout-out. Add proof snippet to active SDR sequences.

**Week 3:** Carousel post. Quote + commentary post. Blog post if applicable.

**Week 4+:** Add to ad rotation. Include in campaign emails. Feed into compilation pieces. Start tracking win rate influence.

---

## What to Measure

Page views are vanity. Track these:

- **Win rate** of deals where the case study was shared
- **Rep mentions** — how often sales references this customer on calls
- **Pipeline influence** — deals that touched this asset before closing
- **Reshares** — did the customer's champion share it to their network?
