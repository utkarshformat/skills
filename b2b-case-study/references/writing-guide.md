# Writing Guide

## Headline

Must contain a specific number. Must name the customer.

**Formulas:**
- "How [Company] [achieved result] with [Product]"
- "How [Company] [verbed] [metric] in [timeframe]"
- "[Company] [verbs] [outcome] with [Product]"

Bad: "How Company X Transformed Their Operations"
Good: "How Company X Cut Onboarding Time by 40% in 3 Months"

---

## Hero Metrics

2-3 stats, displayed big and bold at the top of the page. First thing anyone sees.

Format: `[Number] + [short label]`

Examples: `40%` faster onboarding · `3x` pipeline growth · `15 hrs` saved per month

Every metric must come from the interview or verified data. Include timeframes.

---

## Pull Quote

The single strongest line from the interview. Place it right under the hero metrics with the speaker's name, title, and headshot.

Pick a quote that captures the transformation in one sentence — not generic praise.

---

## Challenge Section

**H2:** "Challenge"
**H3:** A specific subhead that names the actual problem (not "The Challenge")

2-3 short paragraphs + at least one customer blockquote.

**Include:** Business context (growth stage, team constraints), what they were doing before, why that wasn't working, what made this urgent, baseline metrics if available.

**Don't include:** Product mentions — this section is the customer's world before you existed.

**Goal:** Reader finishes this section thinking "that's exactly my situation."

---

## Solution Section

**H2:** "Solution"
**H3:** A subhead framing your product as the response

Break into 2-3 sub-sections, each with a bold header describing a benefit (not a feature). Each sub-section gets a paragraph of narrative + a customer quote.

**Focus on:** How they use it, what changed in their workflow, what surprised them.

**Don't focus on:** Feature lists, technical specs, marketing language.

---

## Results Section

**H2:** "Results"
**H3:** A subhead restating the core outcome with a number

Metrics tied back to the challenge. 2-4 supporting stats. Close with a forward-looking quote about continued value or what they'd tell a peer.

**Metric types that land:**
- Time saved (hours/week, days reduced)
- Revenue or pipeline impact
- Efficiency gains (before → after)
- Scale handled (volume, team size held flat)
- Time-to-value (how fast results showed up)

---

## Company Sidebar

A small panel: Industry | Funding stage | HQ | Team size

Lets readers instantly assess "are they like me?"

---

## Voice Rules

Write like a journalist covering a business. Not a marketer pitching a product.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Let customer quotes carry the story | Write brochure copy |
| Use specific numbers and timeframes | Say "significant improvement" |
| Name tools, competitors, processes | Be vague about context |
| Keep paragraphs to 2-4 sentences | Write walls of text |
| Include honest details about the journey | Present a perfect fairy tale |
| Preserve the customer's natural voice | Polish quotes into ad copy |

**Words to avoid:** "actionable insights," "leverage," "data-driven," "robust," "seamless," "best-in-class," "cutting-edge"

**Test:** If a sentence could appear in any competitor's case study unchanged, rewrite it.

---

## When Metrics Are Limited

Not every customer can share exact numbers. You still need proof.

- Use ranges: "20-30% faster"
- Use relative terms: "cut the time in half"
- Use directional language: "double-digit improvement"
- Focus on strategic outcomes: reduced risk, faster decisions, team confidence

If there are truly no numbers at all, reframe it as a "customer spotlight" and let the narrative carry the weight. But push to get at least one concrete metric — even a rough one makes the difference between a testimonial and a case study.
