# Scoring Rubric — Behavioural Anchors

Score each conversation 0 / 1 / 2 on each dimension. Anchors describe **observable behaviour in the transcript**, not impressions. When a call genuinely gives no opportunity to demonstrate a dimension (e.g. hung up before any pitch was possible, a pure scheduling/holding call, or a correct disqualification), mark it **N/A** and exclude it from that dimension's average rather than scoring 0.

**Disqualification carve-out.** If the prospect reveals a genuine, fit-based non-fit (collection mode the product can't serve, an equivalent owned solution they're committed to, a hard ICP miss) and the rep ends the call efficiently, that is a **correct disqualification, not a cave**: mark **Objection handling** and **Next step** N/A and keep the call out of coaching. Timing / budget / political stalls ("nouveaux élus", "pas avant 2030", "marché en cours") are **stalls, not disqualifiers** — score them normally.

**Display:** every dimension score is shown as `X/2` (`0/2`, `1/2`, `2/2`). The per-call total is shown as `X.X/2.0`.

**Weighted call score** = Σ(dimension score × weight), over the dimensions that were scored (re-normalise across non-N/A weights if any dimension is N/A). Weights sum to 1.0 and each score is 0–2, so the total lands on a **0.0–2.0** scale. Express it as `X.X/2.0`. (Worked example: `0.10×2 + 0.10×2 + 0.20×2 + 0.15×2 + 0.20×1 + 0.20×1 + 0.05×2 = 1.60`, shown as `1.60/2.0`.)

---

## 1. Opening & permission — weight 10%
Does the rep open cleanly, name who they are, and earn the right to keep talking?

- **0** — No name/company given, or launches into pitch with no framing; gets shut down or talked over immediately.
- **1** — Names self/company but rushes past permission; prospect is mildly disoriented about who's calling and why.
- **2** — Names self + Ficha, references prior contact if any, and the prospect is oriented and willing to continue. Survives the gatekeeper without friction.

## 2. Reason-for-call clarity — weight 10%
Is it clear, early, why the rep is calling?

- **0** — Reason never stated plainly, or buried so late the prospect is confused throughout.
- **1** — Reason given but vague or hedged ("je voulais juste faire un point").
- **2** — Clear, specific reason stated in the first turn or two ("je vous rappelle pour voir si vos actions de comm ont réduit le taux d'erreur de tri").

## 3. Discovery / pain — weight 20%
Does the rep uncover the prospect's real situation and pain, or talk at them?

- **0** — No real questions; rep monologues; no information gathered about current process, error rates, or who decides.
- **1** — Asks 1–2 surface questions, accepts the first answer, doesn't probe ("ah ok, d'accord" and moves on).
- **2** — Asks about current sorting process, quantifies (`taux de refus / d'erreur`, tonnage), and probes who decides and on what timeline. Threads the prospect's answers into follow-ups.

## 4. Value framing — weight 15%
Is the offering tied to *this* prospect's situation, or a canned pitch?

- **0** — Generic feature dump (camera, dashboard, cocon) with no link to anything the prospect said.
- **1** — Explains the offering competently but generically; some link to the prospect's context but mostly standard.
- **2** — Explicitly maps the camera/data accompaniment to the prospect's stated pain ("vu que vous avez le bac pucé, la caméra rend l'adresse hyper précise") and references comparable territories.

## 5. Objection handling — weight 20%
When the prospect brushes off, does the rep explore and reframe, or cave / steamroll?

Common Ficha brush-offs: "on est en totale prestation" (collection is outsourced), "pas avant 2030 / futur marché" (procurement cycle not open), "on privilégie les actions terrain."

**The dominant recurring objection family — flag it explicitly.** Live Ficha data shows the single most common stall is procurement-cycle / political timing: newly-elected officials needing time to settle ("les nouveaux élus, il faut qu'ils s'installent"), budget freezes ("en stand-by", "compliqué financièrement de se projeter"), and market-renewal timing ("on vous recontacte en novembre / à la rentrée / pas avant 2030"). This is **structural to selling to French collectivités**, not a per-rep weakness — so when scoring objection handling, watch specifically for how the rep handles this family, and if the *same* family recurs across multiple reps, surface it in the cross-rep patterns section as a team enablement gap (one shared rebuttal to build and drill), not four separate coaching items. A reframe that scores 2 here usually converts the stall into a low-commitment next step the prospect can say yes to *now* despite the timing (e.g. "même si la décision est en novembre, calons 30 min pour que vous ayez la pertinence en tête au moment où vous en parlez aux nouveaux élus").

- **0** — Caves instantly ("ok, pas de souci") and moves to wrap, OR argues/steamrolls without acknowledging.
- **1** — Acknowledges the objection and gives a reasonable response, but doesn't explore the root ("why 2030? what triggers the next marché?").
- **2** — Acknowledges, asks a clarifying question to understand the real constraint, then reframes with relevant proof or a lower-commitment ask (e.g. "même si c'est 2027, calons 30 min pour que vous ayez la pertinence en tête au moment où vous en parlez à l'ADEME").

> Note: in the sample transcript, Simon scored ~1 here — he handled the 2030 objection gracefully and pivoted to a low-commitment meeting ask, but accepted the brush-off rather than probing what would actually move the timeline. That's the difference between a 1 and a 2.

## 6. Next step secured — weight 20%
Does the call end with a real, dated next step?

- **0** — No next step, or "je vous envoie des documents / vous me direz."
- **1** — A next step proposed but soft and undated ("on se rappelle d'ici un an", "éventuellement").
- **2** — A concrete, dated commitment with mutual agreement ("calons 30 minutes la semaine prochaine"), or a clear, time-bound agreed follow-up the prospect assented to.

## 7. Call control & register — weight 5%
Does the rep guide the call and hold a professional French register?

- **0** — Prospect drives throughout; rep reactive; register too casual or fumbling.
- **1** — Mostly guides the call; occasional loss of thread; register acceptable.
- **2** — Steers the call start to finish, handles the gatekeeper smoothly, professional and warm French register throughout.

---

## Scoring discipline
- Score what's **in the transcript**, not what you assume the rep meant.
- A short call is not automatically a low score — a crisp 3-minute call that earns a dated next step can score high.
- A long call is not automatically a high score — rambling counts against discovery and control.
- When in doubt between two scores, pick the lower and note why; the point is honest coaching signal, not flattering averages.
